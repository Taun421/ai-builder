Automation Opportunities Draft

Ranked by: hours reclaimed × how directly it ties to winning new clients. All estimates are drafts.

#1 — Highest impact
Quick Win ~3 hrs/week reclaimed
LinkedIn leads are worked without you thinking about it.
What's happening today
LinkedIn sends 45+ lead suggestions per week. They land in inbox, get reviewed manually (if at all), and mostly go unworked. No outreach follows automatically — each contact requires you to notice, decide, and write a message.
What the automated version looks like
A weekly Claude agent reviews lead suggestions, scores by company size and fit (50+ employees, industries you serve), drafts personalized first-touch LinkedIn messages referencing a specific tool you've built, and queues them for your 30-second review + send. You approve, not compose.
Trigger / output
Trigger: Monday morning weekly batch. Output: 5–10 approved-ready outreach drafts in your inbox, with a one-line note on why each was selected.
Why this is #1
New client acquisition is your top goal. Leads are already arriving — they just aren't being converted. This builds the pipeline directly, with tools you're already building as the hook.
#2
Quick Win ~2 hrs/week reclaimed
Google Alerts become a weekly prospect intelligence brief — not inbox noise.
What's happening today
3–4 Google Alert emails arrive daily across "Benefits Broker", "group benefits", "Employee Benefits Consultant" topics — ~80 emails/month. They're read (maybe) and deleted. No system extracts named companies, relevant news, or talking points.
What the automated version looks like
A Friday Claude agent reads that week's Google Alerts, extracts: (1) companies mentioned as hiring benefits consultants or switching brokers, (2) industry talking points you can use in prospect emails, (3) one "news hook" to open a cold outreach email. Delivered as a single weekly brief.
Trigger / output
Trigger: Friday at 4pm (or on-demand). Output: 1-page brief — top 3 prospect signals, 2 talking points, 1 news hook ready to paste.
Why this matters
You're already investing time reading these alerts. This converts passive reading into active pipeline intelligence with near-zero additional effort.
#3
Medium Lift ~3–4 hrs/week reclaimed
Quote spreadsheeting for renewals happens in minutes, not hours.
What's happening today
When a carrier sends renewal quotes (like the UHC quotes referenced in your Apr 24 email), you manually lay them out side-by-side in the format clients expect. This is a recurring task across your entire book, happening every renewal cycle.
What the automated version looks like
A Claude agent takes attached carrier quote PDFs, extracts plan details, and auto-populates a comparison spreadsheet in your exact preferred format. You review for accuracy, then send. The skill you're already building (the "lifedisabilityspreadsheeting" skill) is most of the way there.
Trigger / output
Trigger: you forward a carrier quote PDF to a Claude agent or drop it in a folder. Output: populated comparison spreadsheet ready for client review.
Effort note
Template and parser need to be built once per carrier format. You've already started this with the UHC spreadsheeting prompt — completing it would unlock a repeatable tool.

#5
Big Build ~4–5 hrs/week reclaimed
Every prospect gets a personalized benefits tool demo before the first meeting.
What's happening today
Building each client benefits site (like the Apr 24 work: benefits page, V2 chatbot, plan guide) takes multiple hours per client and requires manual customization in Claude. It's done after engagement begins, not as a door-opener.
What the automated version looks like
A prospect intake form captures company name, industry, size, and current benefits. Claude auto-generates a branded sample benefits microsite (using your existing skills) and sends it as the first outreach follow-up. The tool IS the pitch — before you've had a single call.
